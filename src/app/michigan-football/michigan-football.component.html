Michigan Football Recruiting Project

The world of college recruiting continues to evolve into a potential A.I. driven competition. In 2018, Wolverine Sports Analytics had the opportunity to work with the Michigan Football Team to try and discover a potential recruiting edge that they could adopt. Under the guidance of J.T. Rogan, Director of Communications & Operations for the Head Coach, we created an algorithm for predicting the likelihood of a recruit committing to Michigan but with a unique approach.

Nearly every high school recruit uses Twitter as a means of making announcements regarding recruiting, so we decided to incorporate self-made Twitter statistics into our algorithm. Along with high school information and official-offer and visit information, our algorithm used percentage of tweets Michigan related and percentage of retweets Michigan related as features.

We tested many forms of machine learning algorithms including: random forest, L1 and L2 logistic regression, and extreme gradient boosting. Ultimately, random forest models worked best for our specific dataset yielding and overall 90.5% accuracy while maintaining an individual-recruit 54.28% accuracy. With a larger dataset and wider assortment of Twitter features one would expect to achieve a higher individual accuracy, so there is room for much improvement.
